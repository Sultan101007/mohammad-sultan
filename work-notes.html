Introduction

What is Work?

"In daily life, work includes any effort, but in science, work is different. Activities like reading, writing, or thinking use energy, but no work is done scientifically."

  • Work is done when a force produces motion in a body.
  • Condition for work done: Force should be applied AND Body should be displaced.
  • If force is applied but there's no displacement, no work is done.
Key Examples:

• A man pushing a stationary rock → No work done (no displacement)

• A man standing with heavy suitcase → No work done (no displacement)

Work Definition

Scientific Definition

Work is done when a force causes displacement in the direction of the applied force.

W = F × s

Where:

  • W = Work done (Joules)
  • F = Force applied (Newtons)
  • s = Displacement in direction of force (meters)

Work Cases & Formula

General Formula

W = F × s × cosθ

Where θ = Angle between Force and Displacement

Case Angle (θ) cosθ Work Done Type
Force & Displacement same direction 0° 1 W = F × s Positive Work
Force perpendicular to Displacement 90° 0 W = 0 Zero Work
Force opposite to Displacement 180° -1 W = -F × s Negative Work

Work Examples

Activity Analysis

Look at the activities listed below. Reason out whether or not work is done:

(i) Suma swimming in a pond: WORK DONE - Force applied causes displacement

(ii) Donkey carrying load: NO WORK - Force vertical, displacement horizontal (90° angle)

(iii) Windmill filling water: WORK DONE - Force lifts water (displacement in force direction)

(iv) Plant photosynthesis: NO WORK - Chemical process, no mechanical displacement

(v) Engine pulling train: WORK DONE - Force causes displacement

(vi) Food grains drying: NO WORK - Heat process, no mechanical displacement

(vii) Sailboat moving: WORK DONE - Wind force causes displacement

Q: Work done by force of gravity on satellite moving round Earth?

ZERO - Gravity force (centripetal) is perpendicular to satellite's displacement (tangential).

Q: Porter lifts 15 kg luggage to 1.5 m height. Calculate work done.

F = mg = 15 × 9.8 = 147 N

W = F × s = 147 × 1.5 = 220.5 J

Work Units

Units of Work

W = F × s = N·m = Joule (J)

1 Joule: Amount of work done when a force of 1 Newton displaces an object by 1 meter in the direction of the force.

SI Unit

Joule (J)

1 J = 1 N·m

CGS Unit

erg

1 erg = 1 dyne·cm

Relation

1 J = 10⁷ erg

Energy

Definition & Forms

"Energy is defined as the ability to do work. Its unit is the same as that of work. Energy is a scalar quantity."

SI unit of energy: Joule (J) = N·m = kg·m²/s²

Forms of Energy:

Kinetic

Due to motion

Potential

Due to position/height

Chemical

Stored in substances

Heat

From hot objects

Electrical

From electric current

Nuclear

From atomic nuclei

Kinetic Energy

Definition & Formula

Kinetic energy is the energy an object has due to its motion.

KE = ½ × m × v²

Where:

  • KE = Kinetic Energy (Joules)
  • m = Mass (kg)
  • v = Velocity (m/s)

Work-Energy Theorem

W = ΔKE = KE_final - KE_initial

"The total work done by forces acting on a particle equals the change in its kinetic energy."

Q: Bullet mass 0.02 kg fired at 400 m/s. Calculate KE.

KE = ½ × 0.02 × (400)²

KE = 0.01 × 160000 = 1600 J

Q: Car (1000 kg) at 30 m/s stops in 50 m. Work by brakes?

Initial KE = ½ × 1000 × 30² = 450,000 J

Final KE = 0

Work = ΔKE = 0 - 450,000 = -450,000 J (negative - braking)

Potential Energy

Gravitational PE

Potential energy due to height/position above ground.

PE = m × g × h

Where:

  • PE = Potential Energy (J)
  • m = Mass (kg)
  • g = Gravity (9.8 m/s²)
  • h = Height (m)

Elastic Potential Energy: Stored in stretched/compressed objects (springs, rubber bands, bows).

PE Examples & Questions

Q: Object mass 10 kg raised to 6 m. Calculate PE. (g=9.8)

PE = mgh = 10 × 9.8 × 6 = 588 J

Q: 200 kg bag raised to what height for PE=9800 J? (g=9.8)

h = PE/(mg) = 9800/(200×9.8) = 9800/1960 = 5 m

Q: Ball (15 kg) thrown up to 5 m. Work by gravity?

Work = ΔPE = mgΔh = 15×9.8×5 = 735 J

Or using energy: PE at top = 735 J, at bottom = 0, work = 735 J

Conservation of Energy

Law of Conservation

"Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only be transformed from one form to another. Total energy of an isolated system remains constant."

Total Energy = KE + PE = Constant

At Top

PE = mgh

KE = 0

Total = mgh

During Fall

PE decreases

KE increases

Total = mgh

At Bottom

PE = 0

KE = ½mv² = mgh

Total = mgh

Energy Conversion Examples

Device/Process Energy Conversion
Torch Chemical → Electrical → Light & Heat
Pendulum KE ↔ PE (constant total)
Hydroelectric Plant Potential → Kinetic → Mechanical → Electrical
Electric Bulb Electrical → Light & Heat
Burning Fuel Chemical → Heat & Light
Electric Heater Electrical → Thermal
Q: Stone (2 kg) released from 100 m. Find KE, PE, total at halfway.

At halfway: h = 50 m

PE = mgh = 2×9.8×50 = 980 J

Total energy = initial PE = 2×9.8×100 = 1960 J

KE = Total - PE = 1960 - 980 = 980 J

Total energy = 1960 J (conserved)

Power

Definition & Formula

Power is the rate of doing work or transferring energy.

P = W / t

Where:

  • P = Power (Watts)
  • W = Work done (Joules)
  • t = Time taken (seconds)

1 Watt: Power required to do 1 Joule of work per second.

1 W = 1 J/s

1 kW = 1000 W = 1000 J/s

Power Problems

Q: Crane lifts 500 kg to 10 m in 20 s. Calculate power.

Work = mgh = 500×9.8×10 = 49,000 J

Power = Work/time = 49,000/20 = 2450 W = 2.45 kW

Q: Lamp consumes 1000 J in 10 s. What is power?

Power = Energy/time = 1000/10 = 100 W

Q: Shobhit (70 kg) climbs 20 m in 20 s. Digraj (60 kg) in 10 s. Who's more powerful?

Shobhit: Work = 70×9.8×20 = 13,720 J, Power = 13,720/20 = 686 W

Digraj: Work = 60×9.8×20 = 11,760 J, Power = 11,760/10 = 1176 W

Digraj is more powerful (1176 W > 686 W)

Commercial Units

Kilowatt-hour (kWh)

Joule is too small for practical energy measurement. Commercial unit is kilowatt-hour.

1 kWh = 1000 W × 3600 s = 3.6 × 10⁶ J

1 unit of electricity = 1 kWh

Used in households and industries for electricity billing.

Q: 60 W bulb used 6 h/day. Energy in 'units' per day?

Energy = Power × time = 60 W × 6 h = 360 Wh = 0.36 kWh = 0.36 units

Practice Questions

Problem Set

Q1: If both mass and velocity are doubled, new KE compared to original?

KE = ½mv²

New KE = ½(2m)(2v)² = ½(2m)(4v²) = 8 × (½mv²)

8 times original KE

Q2: Mass constant, velocity tripled. KE increase?

KE ∝ v²

If v → 3v, then KE → 9×KE

9 times increase (800% increase)

Q3: Two balls: A(5 kg, 15 m/s) and B(10 kg, 10 m/s). Which has more KE?

KE_A = ½×5×(15)² = ½×5×225 = 562.5 J

KE_B = ½×10×(10)² = ½×10×100 = 500 J

Ball A has more KE (562.5 J > 500 J)

Q4: Scooter:bike mass ratio 2:3, same speed 108 km/h (30 m/s). KE ratio?

KE = ½mv², v same for both

KE ∝ m

KE_scooter : KE_bike = m_scooter : m_bike = 2:3

Q5: Increase car velocity from 36 km/h (10 m/s) to 72 km/h (20 m/s). Mass=1500 kg. Work required?

Work = ΔKE = ½m(v₂² - v₁²)

= ½×1500×(20² - 10²) = ½×1500×(400-100)

= ½×1500×300 = 225,000 J = 225 kJ

Multiple Choice Questions

MCQs with Answers

1. Porter with suitcase climbing steps with uniform speed. Work by 'weight of suitcase'?

(a) negative (b) positive (c) zero (d) undefined

(a) negative - Weight acts downward, displacement upward, θ=180°, cos180°=-1

2. Assertion: 10 kg at 6 m has 588 J energy. Reason: Work at height = mgh = energy gained.

(a) Both true, Reason correct

(b) Both true, Reason not correct

(c) Assertion true, Reason false

(d) Assertion false, Reason true

PE = 10×9.8×6 = 588 J ✓

Work done against gravity = mgh = energy stored as PE ✓

(a) Both true, Reason is correct explanation

3. Mass P = 2×Mass Q, Speed P = ½×Speed Q. KE ratio P:Q?

(a) 1:2 (b) 2:1 (c) 1:4 (d) 4:1

KE_P = ½(2m)(v/2)² = ½×2m×v²/4 = ¼mv²

KE_Q = ½mv²

Ratio = (¼mv²):(½mv²) = 1:2

(a) 1:2

4. Masses 1×10³ kg and 4×10³ kg have equal momentum. KE ratio?

a) 2:1 b) 4:1 c) 1:4 d) 1:2

p = mv (same for both)

KE = p²/(2m)

KE ∝ 1/m (since p same)

Ratio = (1/1):(1/4) = 4:1

(b) 4:1

5. Stone falling, PE decreases. What happens to KE?

a) decreases b) same c) increases d) zero

By conservation: PE + KE = constant

If PE decreases, KE must increase

(c) increases
