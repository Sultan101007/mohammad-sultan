Introduction

Food Resources

"All living organisms need food for health, growth and development. Food provides nutrients like carbohydrates, fats, protein, vitamins and minerals."

Green Revolution

Program launched to boost food production using modern technology, irrigation, and improved seeds.

White Revolution

Dairy development programs that boosted milk production (Operation Flood).

Why improvement in food resources?

India's population exceeds one billion and is growing, needing over 250 million tonnes of grain yearly. With limited cultivable land, boosting production efficiency in crops and livestock is essential.

Crop Improvement Approaches

Hybridization

"Process of crossing between two or more genetically dissimilar plants to produce a new variety with good properties of both crops."

  • Intervarietal: Between two varieties of a plant
  • Interspecific: Between two species belonging to same genus
  • Inter Generic: Between species of different genus
Genetically Modified Crops (GMC):

A gene that provides desirable characteristics is introduced, resulting in genetically modified crops.

Good and Healthy Seeds

  • Higher Yield: Increase productivity per acre
  • Improved Quality: Better quality crop products
  • Biotic & Abiotic Resistance: Resistance to diseases, pests, drought, etc.
  • Wider Adaptability: Can grow in different conditions
  • Desired Agronomic Traits: Ideal height, branching, leaves for high production
Q: What is the name given to crops obtained by introducing a gene that provides the desired characteristic?

Answer: Genetically modified crops

Nutrient Management

Soil Macronutrients: N, P, K, Ca, Mg, S
Micronutrients: Fe, Mn, B, Zn, Cu, Mo, Cl
Q: What are macro-nutrients and why are they called macro-nutrients?

Answer: Macro-nutrients are the essential elements which are utilised by plants in large quantities. Since they are required in large quantities, they are known as macro-nutrients. The six macro-nutrients required by plants are nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, calcium, magnesium, and sulfur.

Manure vs Fertilizers

Comparison Table

Cheap, prepared in rural homes/fields Costly, prepared in factories
Slowly absorbed (insoluble in water) Easily absorbed (soluble in water)
Organic Farming:

Farming practice with no artificial chemicals, fertilizers, or pesticides. Relies completely on organic matter like manure, bioagents, biofertilizers, and biopesticides (Neem leaves, turmeric).

Q: How is the use of manure beneficial for our environment?

Answer: The use of manure is beneficial as: 1) Helps recycle biological wastes, 2) Protects environment from chemical damage, 3) Adds organic matter improving soil fertility.

Cropping Patterns

Types of Cropping Patterns

Pattern Definition Examples
Mixed Cropping Growing two or more crops together on same land Wheat + Gram, Groundnut + Sunflower
Intercropping Two or more crops grown in definite pattern (few rows each) Soybean + Maize, Bajra + Cowpea
Crop Rotation Growing different crops one after another on same field Beans → Tomatoes → Corn → Beans
Q: What is the process of growing two or more crops in a definite pattern?

I. Crop rotation II. Inter-cropping III. Mixed cropping IV. Organic cropping

Answer: II. Inter-cropping

Advantages:

Intercropping: Maximizes nutrient use, maintains soil fertility, controls pests naturally.

Crop Rotation: Preserves soil fertility, reduces weeds/pests, reduces chemical fertilizer use, increases yield.

Crop Protection

Pest Control

Pest: Any destructive organism which can destroy or harm crops

  • Weeds: Unwanted plants (e.g., Xanthium)
  • Insects: Cut roots/stems/leaves, suck cell sap
  • Pathogens: Bacteria, fungi, viruses causing diseases

Storage of Grains

Storage Losses:
  • Biotic factors: Insects, rodents, fungi, mites, bacteria
  • Abiotic factors: Moisture, unsuitable temperatures
Preventive Measures:
  • Thorough cleaning before storage
  • Drying in sunlight then shade
  • Fumigation to kill pests

Practice Questions

Short Answer Questions

Q1: If there is low rainfall in a village throughout the year. What measures will you suggest to the farmers for better cropping?

Answer:

  1. Soil moisture conservation using humus and organic manure
  2. Crop rotation and intercropping with varying water needs
  3. Efficient irrigation systems (drip/sprinkler)
  4. Drought resistant crops (millet, sorghum)
  5. Rainwater harvesting and storage
Q2: Chintulal is an organic farmer looking to improve his soil fertility. He wants to know whether he should use organic manure or fertilizers. What would you recommend, and why?

Answer: Organic manure because:

  1. Improves soil fertility and structure without changing pH
  2. Environmentally friendly (recycles waste)
  3. No adverse effects even in large quantities
  4. Cost-effective with long-term benefits
