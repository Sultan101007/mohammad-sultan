Motion

What is Motion?

"An object is said to be in motion when its position changes with time."

  • To describe the position of an object, we need a reference point or origin.
  • Examples: Movement of dust, leaves, branches
  • Phenomena of sunrise, sunset, and changing seasons are due to Earth's motion.
  • Motion is a relative concept - depends on the observer's frame of reference.

Straight line movement

Random Motion
Irregular movement

Rest & Motion

Rest

"A body is said to be in a state of rest when its position does not change with time."

  • Examples: Sleeping, sitting, standing, lying
  • Rest is relative - depends on the observer

Motion is Relative

Key Concept:

A body can be moving for one observer, and at the same time at rest for another observer.

Example:

A person sitting in an airplane is at zero velocity relative to the airplane, but is moving at the same velocity as the airplane with respect to the ground.

Physical Quantities

Scalar vs Vector Quantities

"A physical quantity is a property of a material or system that can be measured and quantified."

Scalar Quantity Vector Quantity
Has only magnitude Has both magnitude and direction
No direction specified Direction is essential
Examples: distance, speed, mass, time Examples: displacement, velocity, acceleration, force
Added algebraically Added vectorially

Distance vs Displacement

Comparison Table

Distance Displacement
Actual length of path travelled Shortest distance between initial and final position
Always positive Can be positive, negative, or zero
Depends on path followed Independent of path followed
Scalar quantity Vector quantity
Q: Can an object have zero displacement if it has moved a certain distance?

Answer: Yes, if an object returns to its starting point, displacement is zero but distance traveled is not zero.

Q: A body is moving along a circular path of radius 7 cm. What is the distance and displacement when it completes half a revolution?

a. Distance: 14 cm, Displacement: 0 cm

b. Distance: 22 cm, Displacement: 7 cm

c. Distance: 22 cm, Displacement: 14 cm

d. Distance: 44 cm, Displacement: 14 cm

Answer: c. Distance: 22 cm, Displacement: 14 cm

Speed & Velocity

Speed

"Speed is the distance traveled by an object in unit time."

Speed = Distance / Time
s = d/t
  • SI Unit: meters per second (m/s)
  • Commercial Unit: km/hr
  • Average Speed: Total distance / Total time

Velocity

"Velocity is the rate of change of displacement or speed with direction."

Velocity = Displacement / Time
v = s/t
Average Velocity = (Initial + Final Velocity)/2
vav = (u + v)/2

Speed vs Velocity

Speed Velocity
Rate at which distance is covered Rate at which position changes with direction
Scalar quantity Vector quantity
Indicates how fast object moves Indicates how fast + direction
Never zero or negative Can be zero, negative or positive
Formula: distance/time Formula: displacement/time
Q: A car travels a distance of 200 km from Delhi to Ambala towards North in 5 hours. Calculate (i) speed, and (ii) velocity.

Answer:

(i) Speed = 200/5 = 40 km/hr

(ii) Velocity = 200/5 = 40 km/hr towards North

Acceleration

Acceleration & Retardation

"Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with time."

Acceleration = (Final Velocity - Initial Velocity) / Time
a = (v - u)/t
  • Positive Acceleration: Velocity increases with time
  • Negative Acceleration (Retardation): Velocity decreases with time
  • SI Unit: m/s²
  • Retardation: Same as acceleration but negative value
Example of Retardation:

A plane landing slows down - this is retardation or deceleration.

Equations of Motion

Three Equations of Motion

v = u + at

Velocity-Time Relation

s = ut + ½at²

Displacement-Time Relation

v² = u² + 2as

Displacement-Velocity Relation

Notation:
  • u = Initial velocity (m/s)
  • v = Final velocity (m/s)
  • a = Acceleration (m/s²)
  • t = Time (s)
  • s = Displacement (m)

Q: a) What can you say about the motion of an object whose distance-time graph is a straight line parallel to the time axis?

Answer: The object is at rest (zero speed).

Q: b) What can you say about the motion of an object if its speed-time graph is a straight line parallel to the time axis?

Answer: The object is moving with constant speed (uniform motion).

Free Fall

Motion Under Gravity

"Free fall motion under gravity is when an object moves only under the influence of gravity, without any other external forces acting upon it."

Key Points:
  • Acceleration due to gravity: g = 9.8 m/s²
  • For free fall, use equations of motion with a = g
  • All objects fall with same acceleration (neglecting air resistance)
  • At maximum height, velocity = 0

Uniform Circular Motion

Circular Motion Basics

"The motion of an object along a circular path covering equal distance along the circumference in the same interval of time."

v = 2πr/t

Velocity for circular path with radius r and time t

  • Examples: Moon around Earth, Earth around Sun, cyclist on circular track
  • Speed remains constant, but direction of velocity changes
  • Even though speed is constant, it's accelerated motion (direction changes)
  • Centripetal acceleration acts towards the center

Numerical Problems

Problem 1: Car Acceleration

Q: A car starts from rest, and accelerates at a constant rate. After 0.25 min, it is moving with a velocity of 25 m/s. At what rate is the car accelerating?

a. 1 m/s² b. 1.33 m/s² c. 1.67 m/s² d. 2 m/s²

Solution:

Given: u = 0, t = 0.25 × 60 = 15 s, v = 25 m/s

Using v = u + at

25 = 0 + a × 15

a = 25/15 = 1.67 m/s²

Answer: c. 1.67 m/s²

Problem 2: Truck Braking

Q: A truck came to rest when the brake was applied for 4s to get retardation of 3 m/s². Calculate how far the truck would have travelled after applying the brake.

a. 6 m b. 12 m c. 24 m d. 48 m

Solution:

Given: v = 0, t = 4 s, a = -3 m/s²

First find u: v = u + at → 0 = u + (-3)×4 → u = 12 m/s

Using v² = u² + 2as

0 = (12)² + 2×(-3)×s

0 = 144 - 6s → 6s = 144 → s = 24 m

Answer: c. 24 m
