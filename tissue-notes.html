Tissues

Definition

"A group of cells having similar shape and function act together to perform a specific function is known as a tissue."

Structural organisation in living organisms:

CELLS → TISSUES → ORGANS → ORGAN SYSTEM → ORGANISM

Q. What is the utility of tissues in multicellular organisms?

Answer: Tissues allow for division of labor, where different groups of cells specialize in specific functions, increasing efficiency in complex organisms.

Plant Tissues

Meristematic vs Permanent Tissue

Meristematic Tissue Permanent Tissue
They have the capacity to divide. They do not have the capacity to divide.
Cells small in size and isodiametric in shape. Cells large in size and their shape varies.
Vacuoles are usually absent. Vacuoles are present in living cells.
Intercellular spaces are generally absent. Visible intercellular spaces are present.
Cell walls are thin. Cell walls can be thin or thick.

Meristematic Tissue

Apical Meristem

  • Found at the tips of stems and roots, and sometimes on leaves.
  • They help the roots and shoots grow longer, making the plant taller.
  • This kind of growth is known as primary growth.

Lateral Meristem

  • It helps the root or stem become thicker.
  • This thickening is known as secondary growth.

Intercalary Meristem

  • Found at the base of leaves or between nodes on branches.
  • It helps parts like the internode or leaves grow longer.
Q. How do meristematic tissues contribute to the healing of wounds in plants?

Answer: Meristematic tissues can divide and differentiate to form new cells that repair damaged areas, helping in wound healing and regeneration.

Permanent Tissue

Characteristics

  • Composed of cells which have lost their capability to divide.
  • Have definite shape, size and thickness.
  • May be dead or living.
  • Formed by division & differentiation of meristematic tissues.
  • Classified into Simple Permanent Tissues and Complex Permanent Tissues.

Parenchyma

  • Support plants and store food in roots and stems.
  • Form the basic packing tissue in plants.
  • Cells have thin walls and are alive.
  • Large gaps between them because they are loosely packed.

Chlorenchyma: Parenchyma with chlorophyll for photosynthesis.

Aerenchyma: Parenchyma with large air spaces for buoyancy.

Collenchyma

  • Found in leaf stalks just below the surface.
  • Provide flexibility, allowing leaves and stems to bend without breaking.
  • Cells are alive, long, and have thick corners.
  • Very little space between these cells.

Sclerenchyma

  • The hard husk of a coconut is made of Sclerenchyma tissues.
  • Found in stems, around vascular bundles, in leaf veins, and hard coverings of seeds and nuts.
  • Provide hardness and stiffness.
  • Cells are dead, long, and narrow with thick walls (lignified).
  • Little to no space between these cells.
Q. How are simple tissues different from complex tissues in plants?

Answer: Simple tissues are made of one type of cells performing similar functions (e.g., parenchyma, collenchyma, sclerenchyma). Complex tissues are made of more than one type of cells working together as a unit (e.g., xylem, phloem).

Complex Permanent Tissue

XYLEM

Also known as wood; a vascular and mechanical tissue. Consists of:

  • Tracheids: Elongated angular dead cells for water conduction (primitive).
  • Vessels: Advanced elements found in angiosperms; cylindrical tube-like structures.
  • Xylem Parenchyma: Small & thick-walled cells for starch storage.
  • Xylem Sclerenchyma: Non-living fibres providing mechanical support.

Except xylem parenchyma, all other xylem elements are dead.

Annual Rings: The annual rings present in the trunk of a tree are xylem rings. By counting them, we can determine the age of a tree.

PHLOEM

Consists of four types of elements:

  • Sieve Tubes: Slender tube-like structures with sieve plates; cytoplasm persists.
  • Companion Cells: Have dense cytoplasm and prominent nuclei; sister cells to sieve tubes.
  • Phloem Fibre: Provide mechanical support to sieve tubes.
  • Phloem Parenchyma: Store food and help in radial conduction of food.

Epidermis & Cork

Epidermis:

  • Protects plants from injury, pathogens, and water loss.
  • In desert plants, thick cuticle reduces water loss.
  • Allows gas exchange during respiration and photosynthesis.

Cork:

  • The outer protective coat of a tree (part of bark).
  • Made up of multiple thick layers from secondary meristem.
  • Protects from infection and prevents water loss.

Animal Tissues

Types of Animal Tissues

  • Epithelial Tissue - Covering and lining tissue
  • Connective Tissue - Support and connection tissue
  • Muscular Tissue - Movement tissue
  • Nervous Tissue - Control and coordination tissue

Epithelial Tissue

Characteristics

  • Always grows on some other types of tissue.
  • Cells are set very close to each other on a non-cellular basement membrane.
  • Consists of single layer of cells.
  • Blood vessels are absent and non-nervous in nature.
  • Covers all organs and lines cavities of hollow organs like stomach.
  • Primarily protective in function.

Types of Epithelium

Type Location
Squamous Epithelium Inner surface of mouth, oesophagus, blood vessels, alveoli (lungs)
Cubical Epithelium Nephron (tubules of kidney), salivary glands
Columnar Epithelium Inner surface of intestine, alimentary canal
Ciliated Epithelium Trachea, Bronchi, Fallopian tubes, Nasal cavity
Stratified Squamous Outer layer of skin

Muscular Tissue

Striated/Skeletal Muscles

  • Voluntary muscles (under the control of one's will).
  • Muscle fibres are multinucleated and unbranched.
  • Each fibre enclosed by sarcolemma membrane.
  • Cytoplasm called sarcoplasm.
  • These muscles get tired and need rest.

Cardiac Muscle Fibres

  • Only involuntary muscles found in the walls of heart.
  • Structure is in between striated and non-striated muscles.
  • Uninucleated and branched; branches united by intercalated disc.
  • Rhythmic contraction and relaxation occurs throughout life.

Non-striated (Smooth) Muscles

  • Involuntary muscles also called smooth muscles.
  • Muscle fibres are uninucleated and spindle shaped.
  • Found in walls of stomach, intestine, urinary bladder, bronchi, iris of eye, etc.
  • Peristaltic movements in alimentary canal are brought about by smooth muscles.

Connective Tissue

Functions

  • Provides structural framework.
  • Connects body parts.
  • Offers protection against infections.
  • Stores fats.
  • Occupies and fills spaces.

Blood

Fluid connective tissue that transports gases, food, hormones, and waste.

  • Plasma (55%): 90-91% water, 7% protein, 0.9% inorganic salts.
  • Corpuscles (45%):
    • RBCs: Erythrocytes with hemoglobin for oxygen transport.
    • WBCs: 'Soldiers of the body' - protect against infections (5 types).
    • Platelets: Spindle-shaped cells for blood clotting.

Tendons Ligaments
Attach muscles to bone Attach bone to bone

White in color Yellow in color

Areolar & Adipose Tissue

Areolar Tissue: Most distributed connective tissue; fills spaces between organs, skin & muscles, around blood vessels.

Adipose Tissue: Made of adipocytes filled with fat; stores excess nutrients, regulates temperature; found beneath skin and around organs.

Q. What are the key types of cartilage, and where are they found in the human body?

Answer: Main types are hyaline cartilage (ends of bones, nose, trachea), elastic cartilage (ear, epiglottis), and fibrocartilage (intervertebral discs, knee meniscus).

Nervous Tissue

Characteristics

  • Highly specialized tissue for perceiving and responding to stimuli.
  • Functional unit is the neuron (nerve cell).
  • Structural and functional unit of nervous system.
  • Longest cell in body.
  • Cell body is called cyton (covered by plasma membrane).

Structure of a Neuron

Part Function
Dendrites Collect information from previous neuron and pass to cell body
Cell Body (Soma) Contains nucleus and organelles
Axon Carries information from cell body to nerve endings
Nerve Endings Transmit signal to next neuron or target cell
Nucleus Contains genetic material
Q. Describe the structure and functions of the central nervous system (CNS).

Answer: CNS consists of brain and spinal cord. Brain controls voluntary actions, thinking, memory. Spinal cord relays messages between brain and body, controls reflex actions.

Plant vs Animal Tissues

Feature Plant Tissues Animal Tissues
Movement Plants are stationary, need strong support Animals move, need tissues for movement
Growth Limited to certain regions Occurs all over the body
Types Meristematic (dividing) and Permanent More specialized, no such distinction
Organization Simple organization Complex organization into organs
Living/Dead Cells Mostly dead cells provide strength Mostly living cells are active
Examples Xylem, Phloem, Meristems Muscles, Blood, Bone

Practice Questions

Fill in the Blanks

  1. The ______ meristem helps to increase the girth of the stem or root. (Lateral)
  2. The process by which cells take up a permanent shape, size, and function is called ______. (Differentiation)
  3. Collenchyma is a ______ tissue present in plants which provides flexibility. (Simple)
  4. The husk of a coconut is composed of ______ tissue. (Sclerenchyma)
  5. Two kidney-shaped cells called ______ enclose the stomata. (Guard cells)
  6. The ______ parenchyma stores food and helps in sideways conduction of water. (Phloem)
  7. Tendon is a connective tissue which connects ______ to bones. (Muscles)
  8. Heart muscle cells are cylindrical and ______. (Branched/Uninucleate)

MCQs

  1. Which of the following tissues has dead cells?
    (b) Sclerenchyma
  2. Girth of stem increases due to
    (b) Lateral meristem

Short Answer Questions

  1. Name the epithelial tissues lining:
    • Blood vessels: Squamous epithelium
    • Small intestine: Columnar epithelium
    • Kidney tubules: Cuboidal epithelium
  2. Where are ciliated epithelial cells found?
    Respiratory tract (trachea, bronchi, nasal cavity)
  3. Give reasons:
    • Meristematic cells lack vacuoles: Because they are actively dividing and don't store materials.
    • No intercellular spaces in sclerenchyma: Due to lignin thickening that makes cells compact.
